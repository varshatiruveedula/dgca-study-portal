What is Airworthiness?
Fitness to fly · Design · Construction · Maintenance · Operation
Core Definition
Airworthiness means an aircraft is fit and safe to fly. When DGCA is satisfied, it issues a Certificate of Airworthiness (C of A).

    Type Certificate (TC)
    Issued by: State of Design (the country where the aircraft was designed, e.g. USA for Boeing)
    ✓Confirms aircraft design complies with airworthiness standards
    ✓Confirms the design is approved
    ✓Can also apply to engine type
    ✓Can also apply to propeller type
    Important: Type Certificate is about the design. A C of A is about a specific aircraft. An aircraft gets a C of A because it is built to an approved Type Certificate.
    International Obligations: ICAO places responsibility on the State of Registry to recognize airworthiness certificates and ensure aircraft meet ICAO standards. Countries should NOT impose stricter requirements on visiting aircraft if ICAO minimums are met.

    C of A Application & Issue Process
    1
    Owner/Operator applies to DGCA
    Application submitted with required documents and evidence of airworthiness.
    2
    DGCA evaluates two conditions
    (a) Required documents/evidence are submitted
    (b) Aircraft is airworthy and safe for operation
    3
    DGCA issues one of two certificates
    ✅ Certificate of Airworthiness
    Aircraft fully meets all airworthiness requirements
    ⚠️ Special Certificate of A/W
    Cannot fully qualify for normal C of A but may safely operate under specified conditions
    Grounds for Suspension / Cancellation of C of A
    A C of A SHALL be suspended if aircraft:
    GroundDescription
    (a) Fails to comply with rules relating to: Operation, Maintenance, Modification, Repair, Replacement, Overhaul, Inspection
    (b) Is modified or repaired contrary to regulations
    (c) Suffers major damage
    (d) Develops a major defect affecting safety
    Critical Rule: Aircraft shall NOT be flown when C of A is suspended or deemed suspended.
    Modifications and Repairs
    Rule: No person shall carry out any modification or repair affecting safety unless:

    (a) It is required under rules, OR
    (b) Prior approval of DGCA is obtained.
    Exam Point: Safety-affecting modifications ALWAYS require DGCA approval. No exceptions.
    🧠
    Memory Trick
    If it affects safety → DGCA must approve it first.
    No approval = illegal modification.
    Safety mod → DGCA approval = Mandatory

    Special Flight Permit & Other Provisions
    When C of A is suspended · Ferry flights · Who applies · Uses
    Special Flight Permit
    !
    C of A is suspended
    Normal flying is now prohibited
    →
    Owner/Operator applies to DGCA
    Application made specifically for a Special Flight Permit
    ✓
    DGCA may issue Special Flight Permit
    Allowing limited, specific flight operations
    Permitted Uses
    ✓Ferry flights — fly to maintenance base
    ✓Maintenance flights — required for upkeep
    ✓Repositioning flights — move to another location
    ✓Inspection flights — for evaluation purposes
    Note: The Special Flight Permit is NOT a blanket permission. It covers only specific, limited operations as approved by DGCA.
    Special Certificate of Airworthiness
    Issued when: Aircraft cannot fully qualify for a normal C of A, but may safely operate under specified conditions.
    Examples of when a Special C of A may be issued:
    →Experimental aircraft
    →Aircraft under development
    →Aircraft not meeting all standards but operationally safe

    ⚠️ DGCA Exam Traps — Chapter 16
    🪤
    Annex 6 vs Annex 8
    Students mix these up. Annex 8 = Airworthiness / Engineering. Annex 6 = Operations. Never swap them.
    🪤
    Type Certificate vs C of A
    TC = issued by State of Design for the type. C of A = issued by DGCA for a specific aircraft. They are not the same.
    🪤
    Special C of A vs Special Flight Permit
    Different documents! Special C of A = aircraft cannot fully qualify. Special Flight Permit = C of A is suspended and only limited ops allowed.
    🪤
    Modification Approval
    Any modification affecting safety requires DGCA approval. Not just "major" modifications — any safety-affecting one.
    🪤
    Who applies for Special Flight Permit?
    The owner/operator applies to DGCA. DGCA may (or may not) issue it. It is not automatic.
