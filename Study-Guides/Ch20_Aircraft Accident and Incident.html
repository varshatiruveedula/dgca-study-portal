Classification of Occurrences
Accident · Serious Incident · Incident · Significant Incident — the #1 exam topic
Level 1 — Most Severe
ACCIDENT
Fatal/serious injury, major aircraft damage, or aircraft missing. Occurred during flight operation.
Level 2
SERIOUS INCIDENT
High probability of accident. Almost became an accident but did not.
Level 3
SIGNIFICANT INCIDENT
Accident or serious incident could have occurred if safety margins had not existed.
Level 4 — Least Severe
INCIDENT
Not an accident. Safety jeopardized but lower impact than accident.
ACCIDENT — Full Definition & Timeframe
Timeframe for manned aircraft:
From: person boards with intention of flight
Until: all persons have disembarked
An occurrence is an ACCIDENT if ANY of these occur:
A
Fatal or Serious Injury — person in aircraft, direct contact, or jet blast exposure
B
Aircraft Damage / Structural Failure — adversely affects structural strength, performance, or flight characteristics
C
Aircraft Missing or Completely Inaccessible

Incident, Significant Incident & Air Traffic Incident
Incident: An occurrence NOT qualifying as an accident. Safety jeopardized but lower impact.
Examples of Incidents:
→Forced landing
→Landing with unserviceability
→Aircraft position unknown
→Aircraft damaged below accident threshold
Significant Incident: Accident or Serious Incident could have occurred — safety margins existed and prevented it.
Air Traffic Incident (AIRPROX): Serious occurrence related to ATS. Aircraft come dangerously close.
ATS Difficulties caused by: faulty procedures · non-compliance · ground facility failure

AIRPROX = Aircraft Proximity Event. Aircraft come dangerously close. Classified as a Serious Incident if avoidance action was required.

Requires immediate reporting on air-ground frequency

Reporting Flow — Step by Step
1
During flight — Report immediately
Transmit initial radio report on the appropriate air-ground frequency. Include: Aircraft identification · Type of incident · Details · Miscellaneous information
2
After landing — Notify within 24 hours
Notification sent as soon as practicable and not later than 24 hours after becoming aware. Use fastest available communication method.
3
Notify AAIB and DGCA
Primary notifications go to AAIB and DGCA.
4
If accident occurred in India — Additional notifications
Also inform: District Magistrate and Nearest Police Station.
5
Submit full incident report to DGCA
Written report within 24 hours after landing.

Aircraft shall NOT be disturbed UNLESS one of these conditions applies:
✓
Rescue Operations: To save lives, rescue injured, or prevent further damage
✓
Water Accident: To move aircraft to a safe place
✓
Cargo Removal: Allowed under AAIB supervision
✓
Passenger Baggage: Allowed after photography, weighing, documentation — under supervision

⚠️ Top DGCA Exam Traps — Chapter 20
🪤
Fatal Injury time limit
Death must occur within 30 days to count as a fatal injury from that accident. After 30 days, it is NOT counted as a fatal injury from the accident.
🪤
Serious Injury hospitalization
Must be hospitalized for MORE THAN 48 hours and within 7 days of the injury. Both conditions must be met.
🪤
Fractures that are NOT Serious Injury
Fractures of fingers, toes, and nose do NOT qualify as serious injury. Any other bone fracture qualifies.
🪤
Tire/brake damage is NOT an accident
Damage limited to: engine cowling, propeller, wing tips, antennas, tires, brakes, wheels, fairings, panels, landing gear doors, windscreen, bird strike, hail — these are NOT accidents.
🪤
Fire even if extinguished = Serious Incident
Any fire or smoke in cockpit, cabin, cargo, or engine — even if extinguished — is a Serious Incident. The fact it was put out does not downgrade it.
🪤
CVR/FDR protection
CVR/FDR data cannot be used for disciplinary, civil, or criminal proceedings. This is intentional — to encourage open reporting culture without fear of prosecution.
🪤
Notification — who and when
Notify AAIB + DGCA within 24 hours. Also notify District Magistrate and nearest Police Station if accident in India. PIC, owner, operator, hirer or person-in-charge all have this obligation.
