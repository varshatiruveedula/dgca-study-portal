DGCA Traps
ROGER ≠ YES · WILCO ⊇ ROGER · STANDBY ≠ clearance · GO AHEAD = deleted
⚠️ Key DGCA Trap Questions
🪤
YES in ICAO = ?
AFFIRM — NOT "Roger". Roger only means "message received".
🪤
ROGER WILCO is wrong
WILCO already includes ROGER. Saying "Roger Wilco" is redundant and incorrect.
🪤
STANDBY is not a clearance
STANDBY means wait. It is NOT approval and NOT denial. Pilot must wait for further instruction.
🪤
GO AHEAD is deleted
ICAO no longer uses "GO AHEAD". This phrase has been deleted from standard phraseology.
🪤
MONITOR vs CONTACT
MONITOR = listen only, no call needed. CONTACT = must establish two-way communication.

Radio Theory & Wave Propagation
Speed of light · f=c/λ · Frequency bands · Ground wave · Sky wave · LOS
Core Radio Theory
3×10⁸
m/s
Speed of all EM waves (speed of light)
f = c/λ
Frequency = Speed ÷ Wavelength
f↑ λ↓
Frequency & wavelength are inversely proportional
Polarisation = Direction of the electrical field. EM wave has electrical and magnetic components, both perpendicular to each other and to direction of travel.
Refraction rule: Radio waves entering denser medium bend toward the normal. Entering less dense medium → bend away from normal.

Wave Propagation Types
🌍 Ground Wave
→Travels along Earth's surface
→Used by LF and MF
→Long range · Follows earth curvature
🌌 Sky Wave
→Refracted/reflected by ionosphere
→Used by MF (partly) and HF
→Very long range · Oceanic HF comms
LOS (Line of Sight) Range formula: Range = 1.25 × (√Ht + √Hr) nautical miles, where Ht = transmitter height (ft), Hr = receiver height (ft). Higher antenna = greater range.

⚠️ Top DGCA Exam Traps — Chapter 19
🪤
QDM vs QDR — TO vs FROM
QDM = magnetic heading TO station (Direct Me). QDR = magnetic bearing FROM station (like a radial). Very common mix-up.
🪤
ROGER ≠ YES
ROGER only means "message received". AFFIRM = YES. Using ROGER instead of AFFIRM is a classic wrong answer in DGCA MCQs.
🪤
WILCO includes ROGER
WILCO = "received and will comply." Since it includes received, saying "Roger Wilco" is incorrect.
🪤
Flight number call signs have NO abbreviated form
Air India 809 stays Air India 809. Cannot be abbreviated to AI 09 or India 809.
🪤
406 MHz ELT is satellite-monitored
ELT transmits on 121.5 / 243 / 406 MHz. Only 406 MHz is satellite-monitored.
🪤
CPDLC is NOT for urgent messages
CPDLC is for non-urgent ATC messages only. Voice remains primary for urgent/critical communications.
🪤
VOR vs VHF COM frequency overlap
VOR = 108–117.95 MHz. VHF COM = 118–137 MHz. Both VHF band but different frequency ranges.
🪤
ILS Glide Slope is UHF not VHF
Localizer = VHF (108–111.95 MHz). Glide Slope = UHF (329–335 MHz). Different bands despite both being ILS components.
