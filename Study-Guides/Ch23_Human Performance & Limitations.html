Core Definitions
HP

HL
Human Limitations — Physical and psychological factors that reduce performance and may lead to errors
HF
Human Factors — Study of human capabilities, limitations, and interaction between humans and aviation systems
Why Human Factors Matters

Key Terms Defined
SA
Situational Awareness — Knowing what is happening around you, now and in the future. The pilot's picture of the world.
CRM
Crew Resource Management — Effective use of all available resources (people, information, equipment) to ensure safe flight.
TEM
Threat and Error Management — Proactively identifying threats and errors before they develop into accidents.
CFIT
Controlled Flight Into Terrain — Aircraft flown under control into terrain due to pilot error or loss of situational awareness.

⚠️ Top DGCA Exam Traps — Chapter 23
🪤
Anthropometry definition
Anthropometry = Study of human body SIZE, SHAPE and MEASUREMENTS — specifically for cockpit/seat design. Often confused with physiology (body functions).
🪤
>73% vs other figures
Human factors contribute to MORE THAN 73% of accidents. Sometimes worded as "70%" in options — the correct answer per ICAO/DGCA notes is >73%.
🪤
CFIT — aircraft is NOT malfunctioning
In CFIT, the aircraft is airworthy and under full control. The accident happens because the crew is unaware of terrain. It is NOT a technical failure.
🪤
No oxygen = 5–8 minutes (not 3–4)
Complete absence of oxygen causes death in approximately 5–8 minutes. Don't confuse with time of useful consciousness at altitude (which is different).
🪤
Immediate vs later altitude response
Immediate = ↑ Breathing rate (fast response). Later = ↑ Red blood cell production (slow, takes days/weeks). Students often confuse the order.
🪤
Sociology vs Psychology discipline
Sociology = groups, CRM, teamwork, crew behaviour. Psychology = individual mind, decisions, stress, pilot selection. CRM belongs to Sociology, not Psychology.
