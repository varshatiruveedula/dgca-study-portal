Top Exam Traps
🪤
Head-on = BOTH turn RIGHT (not just one aircraft)
In a head-on situation, BOTH aircraft turn right. Not just one. Similarly, in taxi head-on both stop or alter course to the right.
🪤
Radio failure radar = 7 min · Non-radar = 20 min (not swapped)
Radar = 7 minutes. Non-radar = 20 minutes. Examiner frequently swaps these. RVSM = FL290-410 (not FL200 or FL250).
🪤
Anti-collision lights required DAYTIME too (if fitted)
Common wrong answer: "only at night." Anti-collision lights (beacon, strobes) must be used even in daylight if the aircraft is fitted with them.
🪤
SVFR = 1500m · Helicopter SVFR = 1000m (not 1500m for both)
Standard SVFR = 1500m visibility minimum. Helicopter SVFR = 1000m. The helicopter exception has lower visibility.
🪤
ETA change = >2 min · Delay = ≥15 min (different thresholds)
ETA change requiring notification = MORE THAN 2 minutes. Flight plan delay requiring notification = 15 MINUTES OR MORE. Two different triggers.
🪤
Cannot cancel IFR immediately upon entering VMC
Pilot SHALL NOT cancel IFR merely because VMC is encountered. Must intend to remain in VMC for a reasonable period before cancelling IFR.
