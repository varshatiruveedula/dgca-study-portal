⚠️ Top Exam Traps
🪤
1 min = DEP/DEP only. 3 min = DEP vs ARR (don't mix)
1 minute is ONLY for departure vs departure (diverging 45°). 3 minutes is departure vs arrival with 45° divergence. These are completely different situations.
🪤
Tailwind = 2 kt (lowest, not highest)
Tailwind is the smallest threshold (2 kt) because it is the MOST critical — it extends landing distance. Students instinctively choose 10 kt for tailwind. Wrong! Tailwind=2, Crosswind=5, Headwind=10.
🪤
EAT at ≥10 min, Revised EAT at ≥5 min, Holding ≥30 min
Three separate EAT triggers — all commonly mixed up. Issue EAT if delay ≥10min. Revise EAT if it changes ≥5min. Transmit EAT if holding ≥30min even without specific delay.
🪤
Terrain clearance = PILOT (not ATC) during visual approach
Once ATC issues visual approach clearance, ATC no longer maintains terrain clearance responsibility — it transfers entirely to the PILOT. This is a direct DGCA trap.
🪤
Own separation in VMC: daylight + Class D/E + ≤10,000ft
All THREE conditions must be met simultaneously. Not at night, not above 10,000ft, not in Class A/B/C. Missing any one of them invalidates the own-separation clearance.
🪤
OCT ≠ EAT. OCT = leave fix. EAT = start approach.
OCT = Onward Clearance Time = when to LEAVE the holding fix. EAT = when to START the approach. OCT comes BEFORE EAT in the sequence. Classic DGCA swap trap.
