Licensing Authority in India
DGCA / Central Government is the licensing authority in India. It may grant, renew, vary, or refuse any licence or rating.
Common trap: ICAO sets standards; AAI manages airports. Only DGCA / Central Government issues pilot licences.

    ⚠️ Common DGCA Exam Traps
    🪤
    SPL Exam Type
    SPL exam is ORAL, not written or computer-based.
    🪤
    Instrument Time CPL vs CPL+IR
    Normal CPL = 10 hrs | CPL with IR = 50 hrs. Don't mix them up.
    🪤
    IR Instrument Time
    IR requires 40 hrs instrument time (not 20). Recent = 5 hrs. Validity = 12 months (not 6).
    🪤
    Night T/Os & Landings
    PPL night rating = 5+5 | CPL = 10+10.
    🪤
    Age 60–65 Rule
    Age 60–65 = CAN fly commercial but only in multi-crew with other pilot below 60. Age 65+ = PPL privileges only.
    🪤
    Renewal Window Rule
    If IR or Proficiency Check is renewed within 2 months before expiry, validity runs from previous expiry date (not test date).
