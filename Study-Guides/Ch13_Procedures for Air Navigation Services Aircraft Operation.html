⚠️ Top DGCA Exam Traps
🪤
PDG formula — 2.5 + 0.8 = 3.3
Standard PDG = 3.3% = OIS (2.5%) + obstacle margin (0.8%). MOC at DER = ZERO. After DER, MOC increases by 0.8%.
🪤
Sector 1 = Parallel (NOT Direct)
Sector 1 = Parallel. Sector 2 = Offset/Teardrop. Sector 3 = Direct. Students often confuse Sector 1 and Sector 3.
🪤
TA = Look only, RA = Act immediately
TA = Traffic Advisory = increase awareness, NO manoeuvre. RA = Resolution Advisory = IMMEDIATELY comply even against ATC instruction.
🪤
NPA uses MDA/MDH (level off), PA uses DA/DH (no level off)
NPA descends to MDA then levels off. Precision approach descends on glideslope to DA — still descending when decision is made.
🪤
FAF vs FAP — different for NPA and PA
FAF = Final Approach FIX (used in NPA). FAP = Final Approach POINT on glideslope (used in ILS/PA). Easy to mix up.
🪤
Holding timing starts ABEAM fix (not immediately after crossing)
Start timing when ABEAM the fix OR when outbound turn completed — whichever is LATER. Not when you cross the fix.
