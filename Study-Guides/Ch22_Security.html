What is Annex 17?
Full Name
Security — Safeguarding International Civil Aviation Against Acts of Unlawful Interference
Introduced after: Increase in aviation crimes and hijackings. Originally focused on hijacking (before 1985), then expanded significantly.
Annex 17 protects against:
✓Hijacking
✓Sabotage
✓Bomb threats
✓Terrorist attacks
✓Unlawful seizure of aircraft
✓Other unlawful interference

⚠️ Top DGCA Exam Traps — Chapter 22
🪤
Annex 17 vs Annex 13 vs Annex 9
Annex 17 = Security (unlawful interference). Annex 13 = Accident Investigation. Annex 9 = Facilitation. Very commonly mixed up in MCQs.
🪤
Re-screening — BOTH passenger AND cabin baggage
When screened passengers mix with unscreened passengers, BOTH the passenger AND their cabin baggage must be re-screened. Selecting just "passenger" or just "baggage" is wrong.
🪤
Inquiry Officer minimum rank
Inquiry Officer must be at least Assistant Commissioner of Security. Not just "any security officer".
🪤
Aircraft (Security) Rules year
The year is 2011. Not 2001, not 2015. This is a specific number DGCA tests.
🪤
4 parties notified after unlawful interference
State of Registry + State of Operator + States of affected citizens (died/injured/hostage) + ICAO. Students forget ICAO or forget the "affected states" element.
🪤
Commissioner of Security vs DG BCAS
DG BCAS = head of BCAS (the organisation). Commissioner of Security (Civil Aviation) = responsible for Annex 17 implementation and receives security incident reports. They are different roles.
