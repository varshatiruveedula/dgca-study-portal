Murphy's Law
If equipment CAN be used incorrectly, sooner or later it WILL be. Design must minimize error opportunities.
8 Control Design Principles
A
Standardization — Consistent location & operation across types
B
Frequency of Use — Often-used controls easiest to reach
C
Sequence of Use — Controls in sequence → arranged in sequence
D
Importance — Critical controls easily accessible and unobstructed
E
Visual/Tactile Dissimilarity — Controls must LOOK and FEEL different to prevent selection errors
F
Symbolism — Control shape indicates function (gear lever = wheel shape)
G
Simultaneous Use — Controls used together → located together
H
Warnings — Get attention without causing excessive startle

⚠️ DGCA Exam Traps
🪤
STM = 7±2 items (NOT 5 or 9)
Short-Term Memory capacity = 7 ± 2 items = 5 to 9 items maximum. Duration = 10-20 seconds. Use chunking to extend.
🪤
Automation RELOCATES — does NOT remove — error
Critical distinction. Automation changes the TYPE of errors pilots make (from manual to monitoring/programming errors), not eliminating them.
🪤
Free-running = 25 hrs (NOT 24)
Natural circadian rhythm without external time cues = ~25 hours. Normal (with environmental cues) = 24 hours.
🪤
East→West = Easier jet lag adaptation
West→East travel is 50% SLOWER to adapt. East→West is easier. Common mix-up.
🪤
REM = Paradoxical Sleep (not deep sleep)
REM = Rapid Eye Movement = Paradoxical Sleep (brain active like wakefulness). Deep sleep = NREM Stages 3 & 4. Dreams occur in REM.
🪤
NREM Stage 2 = 50% of sleep
Most sleep time is spent in NREM Stage 2 (light sleep). Deep sleep = Stages 3 & 4 but less total time. Stage 2 dominates.
