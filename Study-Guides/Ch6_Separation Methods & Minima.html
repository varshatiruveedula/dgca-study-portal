⚠️ Top Exam Traps
🪤
5 min = 20 kt · 3 min = 40 kt (not the other way round)
Examiner frequently swaps these. 20kt advantage = 5 min. 40kt advantage = 3 min. Greater speed advantage = SHORTER separation needed. Know it instantly.
🪤
RVSM = 1000ft (same as below FL290, not 2000ft)
In RVSM airspace, even above FL290, separation is 1000 ft between RVSM-approved aircraft. Without RVSM approval above FL290 = 2000 ft.
🪤
Class C: IFR↔IFR AND IFR↔VFR (not VFR↔VFR)
Class C provides separation between IFR↔IFR AND IFR↔VFR. VFR↔VFR is NOT separated in Class C. This catches many students.
🪤
VOR=15° · NDB=30° (larger angle = less accurate aid)
NDB is less accurate than VOR, so requires a WIDER divergence angle (30°) for lateral separation. VOR = 15°. Both require the aircraft to be at least 15 NM from the station.
🪤
Same track = less than 45° (not 90° or 135°)
Same track means angular difference is LESS THAN 45° or MORE THAN 315°. Common wrong answer: "less than 90°". The cutoff is 45°, not 90°.
🪤
Radar/ADS-B uses the SAME distances (5NM ≤60NM, 10NM >60NM)
ADS-B separation follows exactly the same distances as radar: 5 NM within 60 NM, 10 NM beyond 60 NM. Identical rules for both systems.
