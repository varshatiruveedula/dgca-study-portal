Top Exam Traps
🪤
VT = Civil India · VU = Military India (not VU for civil)
Indian civilian aircraft = VT (e.g. VT-ABC). Indian military = VU. Students sometimes write VU for civil. VT is the internationally assigned ITU code for India's civil aviation.
🪤
C of R issued by CENTRAL GOVERNMENT (not DGCA directly)
C of R is issued by the Central Government through DGCA. The issuing authority is Central Government — DGCA is the executing agency. The aircraft register is maintained by DGCA (Director General).
🪤
Lessor = Owner (gives aircraft) · Lessee = Operator (uses aircraft)
Lessor is the OWNER who gives the aircraft. Lessee is the OPERATOR who uses it. Frequently reversed in exam options. Memory: LesSOR = SORce (gives). LesSEE = SEEs it daily (uses).
🪤
ID Plate = Metal OR Fireproof (not just metal)
The identification plate must be made of metal OR other fireproof material. Not exclusively metal — fireproof material is also acceptable. Near main entrance.
🪤
CofA expired 5 YEARS → registration cancelled (not 1 or 3 years)
If the Certificate of Airworthiness has been expired for 5 years or more, DGCA may cancel the registration. The threshold is specifically 5 years.
🪤
GIFT City = Gandhinagar (not Ahmedabad or Mumbai)
GIFT City (Gujarat International Finance Tec-City) is in GANDHINAGAR, not Ahmedabad. Both are in Gujarat — but the answer is specifically Gandhinagar.
