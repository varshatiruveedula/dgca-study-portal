Key Definitions — Annex 9
Cargo · Deportee · Direct Transit · Embarkation · Disembarkation · Loading · Unloading
Core Definitions — Learn All of These
CARGO
Any property carried on an aircraft OTHER THAN: Mail · Stores · Accompanied baggage · Mishandled baggage
🧠 Cargo = property EXCLUDING mail, stores, baggage
DEPORTEE
A person who legally or illegally entered a State and was later ordered to leave by competent authority
🧠 Deportee = ordered OUT by authorities
DIRECT TRANSIT AREA
Special airport area where transit passengers remain without entering the country. No immigration formalities.
🧠 Direct Transit = stays airside, no country entry
EMBARKATION
Boarding an aircraft to commence a flight
🧠 Embark = get ON aircraft
DISEMBARKATION
Leaving an aircraft after landing
🧠 Disembark = get OFF aircraft
LOADING
Placing cargo, mail, baggage, or stores onto an aircraft
🧠 Loading = putting things ON
UNLOADING
Removing cargo, mail, baggage, or stores from an aircraft after landing
🧠 Unloading = taking things OFF
INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT
Airport designated for entry/departure of international traffic where Customs, Immigration, Public Health, and Quarantine formalities are carried out
🧠 4 formalities = Customs+Immigration+Health+Quarantine
CLEARANCE OF GOODS
Completion of customs formalities allowing goods to enter a country, be exported, or be placed under another customs procedure
COMMISSARY SUPPLIES
Items used for passenger comfort and catering: Food · Drinks · Disposable service items
🧠 Commissary = aircraft catering supplies
COMMENCEMENT OF JOURNEY
The point where a passenger begins the journey. Transit passengers continuing the journey remain on the same journey if staying inside the transit area.

Indian National Provisions
First landing rule · Customs aerodrome · Departure clearance · 111 Rule · 6-month exemption
First Landing & Departure Rules — India
First Landing
Aircraft entering India must first land at an International Aerodrome
Last Departure
Aircraft leaving India must make final departure from an International Aerodrome
Customs Aerodrome: Aircraft may also land at or depart from any notified Customs Aerodrome.
Domestic Pick-up Restriction: International flights are NOT permitted to pick up passengers at one Indian city and drop them at another Indian city without special authorization.
🧠
India Entry/Exit Rule
FIRST IN = International Aerodrome  |  LAST OUT = International Aerodrome

⚠️ Top DGCA Exam Traps — Chapter 21
🪤
Cargo does NOT include mail and baggage
Cargo = property on aircraft EXCLUDING: mail, stores, accompanied baggage, mishandled baggage. DGCA loves testing this definition.
🪤
Passenger Manifest is NOT normally required
States shall NOT normally require presentation of a Passenger Manifest. Very counter-intuitive and frequently asked.
🪤
CMC is NOT a licence
The Crew Member Certificate (CMC) is primarily an identification document — it is NOT a licence. Students often confuse it with a pilot licence or medical certificate.
🪤
Max 3 copies of any aircraft document
States shall NOT require more than 3 copies of aircraft documents. The actual India rule is 1 copy each (GENDEC + Pax Manifest + Cargo Manifest) per authority.
🪤
Cargo must be presented 4 working hours before departure
4 working hours — not 2, not 6. Specifically for export cargo including unaccompanied baggage.
🪤
6 months = customs duty exemption for foreign aircraft
A foreign aircraft not registered in India and leaving within 6 months is exempt from customs duty — provided a written declaration is submitted on arrival.
🪤
GENDEC routing must include every en-route stop
GENDEC flight routing must show: Origin → every en-route stop → Destination. Not just origin and destination.
