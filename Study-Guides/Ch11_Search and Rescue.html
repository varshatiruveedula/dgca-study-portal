Pilot Actions at Accident Scene
If pilot sees craft in distress — MUST:
1Keep distress craft in sight
2Determine position
3Inform RCC / ATS
First aircraft (non-SAR) at scene: Takes charge of on-scene operations until SAR aircraft arrives.
When distress transmission intercepted:
→Acknowledge distress message
→Record position
→Take bearing
→Inform RCC / ATS
→Proceed to position if appropriate

How COSPAS-SARSAT Works — Step by Step
1
ELT/EPIRB transmits on 406 MHz when activated
2
Satellite receives signal and forwards to LUT (Local User Terminal)
3
LUT passes data to Mission Control Centre (MCC)
4
MCC alerts RCC — SAR operation launched

⚠️ Top DGCA Exam Traps
🪤
V vs X — Most Confused
V = Require Assistance (general). X = Require MEDICAL Assistance. X looks like a medical cross.
🪤
LL vs LLL
LL = Found ALL personnel. LLL = Operation Completed. These are different — don't confuse!
🪤
NASARCC Chairman vs NMSARB Chairman
NASARCC = Secretary, Ministry of Civil Aviation. NMSARB = DG, Indian Coast Guard. Common exam mix-up.
🪤
Night acknowledgement — no landing lights
Normal = Flash landing lights × 2. If NO landing lights → Flash NAVIGATION lights × 2.
🪤
Mumbai and Chennai appear in BOTH lists
Mumbai + Chennai = both RCC and MRCC locations. Port Blair = MRCC only. Delhi + Kolkata = RCC only.
