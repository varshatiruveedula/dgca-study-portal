Top Exam Traps
🪤
Article 38 = Differences (NOT SARPs adoption)
Article 37 = ICAO adopts SARPs. Article 38 = State MUST notify ICAO if it cannot comply with a Standard. Both are asked. Don't mix 37 and 38.
🪤
IASTA ratified by India · IATA NOT ratified by India
Both signed at Chicago 1944. India ratified IASTA (technical freedoms 1+2) but NOT IATA (commercial freedoms 3+4+5). Common trap: swapping which is ratified.
🪤
Hague=1970 Hijacking · Montreal=1971 Aviation Safety (not same year)
Hague 1970 = Hijacking specifically. Montreal 1971 = broader safety (bombs, violence, navigation interference). One year apart but completely different scope.
🪤
ANC = 19 members (not 36 like Council)
Council = 36 contracting States. Air Navigation Commission = 19 members appointed by Council. Committee on Unlawful Interference = 15. These three numbers must be instant.
🪤
Annexes 2,5,7,8 = Standards ONLY (no Recommended Practices)
Only four Annexes have Standards without Recommended Practices: 2=Rules, 5=Units, 7=Registration, 8=Airworthiness. Memory: Rules, Units, Registration, Airworthiness.
🪤
Rome Convention = India NOT ratified (ground damage)
India has NOT ratified the Rome Convention 1952 which deals with damage to persons/property on the ground. All other major conventions listed here are ratified by India.
